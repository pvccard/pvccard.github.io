The Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) is a statutory authority established under the provisions of the Aadhaar Act, 2016. It was created to issue Unique Identification numbers (UID), named "Aadhaar", to all residents of India. This website serves as an informational blog to help you navigate the official services.

How to Download e-Aadhaar

Carrying a physical card is no longer necessary. The e-Aadhaar is a digitally signed, legally valid PDF.

  • Quick Download: Visit the MyAadhaar Portal (myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in) and enter your Aadhaar Number or VID.
  • Regular Version: Shows your full 12-digit number (Ideal for Banking/Govt).
  • Masked Version: Hides the first 8 digits (Ideal for Hotels/Travel privacy).
  • PDF Password: First 4 letters of your name (CAPS) + Year of Birth (e.g., RAVI1990).

Mandatory Document Update

If your Aadhaar was issued more than 10 years ago, you must "re-verify" it to keep your records active.

  • Free Deadline: Extended until 14 June 2026 for all online updates.
  • Why Update: Ensures bank KYC, Ration card, and PAN links remain functional.
  • Online Only: Free via the portal. Physical centers charge a fee of ₹50.
  • Requirements: Upload Proof of Identity (PAN/Voter ID) and Proof of Address (Bills/Rent Agreement).

How to Check Your Aadhaar Status Instantly

You no longer need to visit a center to track your application progress.

  • Aadhaar Mitra: Use the AI Chatbot on the UIDAI site for instant replies via Enrollment ID (EID).
  • URN Tracking: Enter your Update Request Number on the "Check Status" page.
  • Completed Status: Download your new card immediately once the status shows "Completed".
  • Rejected Status: Review the mismatch reason provided and re-upload documents.

Order the Official Aadhaar PVC Card (₹50 Only)

Stop using laminated paper copies. UIDAI now issues a secure, weather-proof PVC Aadhaar Card that fits in your wallet like an ATM card.

  • Durable & Secure: Waterproof and tear-resistant. Contains a digitally signed QR code, hologram, and ghost image.
  • Legally Valid: This is the official version issued by UIDAI and is accepted everywhere in India.
  • Order Process: Go to the MyAadhaar Portal and click "Order Aadhaar PVC Card" using your 12-digit UID.
  • No Registered Mobile? You can use any alternate mobile number to receive the OTP for the order.
  • Fee & Delivery: Pay ₹50 online (UPI/Card). The card is delivered to your registered address via Speed Post within 5-10 days.

Lost Your Aadhaar? Retrieve Your Number Instantly

If you lost your card and don't remember your 12-digit number, do not enroll again. You can recover it online in minutes.

  • Retrieve EID/UID: Visit the portal and select "Retrieve EID/UID".
  • Exact Details: Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your Aadhaar records.
  • Verification: Provide your registered Mobile Number or Email Address to receive an OTP.
  • SMS Delivery: Once verified, your Aadhaar Number (UID) is sent immediately to your phone via SMS.
  • Next Step: Use the retrieved number to download your digital e-Aadhaar PDF.

Skip the Queue: Book an Appointment Online

Biometric updates (Photo/Fingerprints) and Mobile linking require a physical visit. Book a slot to save time.

  • Guaranteed Slot: Avoid long queues and the risk of being turned away due to crowds.
  • Aadhaar Seva Kendra (ASK): Visit appointments.uidai.gov.in to find a state-of-the-art center in your city.
  • Process: Log in with your mobile number, select your city, and pick a preferred Date and Time slot.
  • Appointment Slip: Download and carry the slip to the center to go directly to the verification counter.

Troubleshooting Aadhaar Issues

  • "Duplicate Found": Your biometrics exist already. Call 1947.
  • Biometric Mismatch: Update biometrics at a center or use iris/face auth.
  • Request Rejected: Check the rejection reason code and fix documents.

Linking Aadhaar with PAN

Aadhaar Enabled Payment System (AePS)

Allows banking (Withdrawal, Deposit, Statements) via Bank Name, Aadhaar Number, and Fingerprint at PoS machines. Safety Tip: Lock biometrics if not using AePS.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Can someone hack my Bank Account if they know my Aadhaar Number?

Concern: With Aadhaar linked to bank accounts, many fear that sharing the 12-digit number makes them vulnerable to theft.

Answer: Just knowing your Aadhaar number is NOT enough to hack your bank account. A financial transaction requires a second layer of authentication—either your OTP (sent to your phone) or your Biometrics (fingerprint/iris).

Safety Tip: To be 100% safe, use the "Biometric Lock" feature in the MyAadhaar app. This ensures that even if a scammer clones your fingerprint (for AePS fraud), the transaction will fail.

2. I lost my registered mobile number. Can I update it online to get the OTP?

Concern: Users are locked out of the MyAadhaar portal because they no longer possess the SIM card linked to their Aadhaar.

Answer: No. You cannot update your mobile number online. This is a strict security measure to prevent identity theft.

Solution: You must physically visit the nearest Aadhaar Enrollment Center. You do not need documents to update your mobile number; only carry your Aadhaar card. Update takes 24-48 hours.

3. I have already updated my Name/DOB once. Can I change it again?

The Answer: UIDAI has strict lifetime limits on demographic updates:

NameMax 2 times
GenderOnly 1 time
Date of BirthOnly 1 time

The Solution for Limit Exceeded: You must visit a UIDAI Regional Office in person with valid proofs and apply through the "Exception Handling Process."

4. My Aadhaar is linked to my bank, but I am not receiving Government Subsidies (DBT). Why?

Answer: Simply giving your Aadhaar copy to the bank is not enough. You must specifically request the bank to "Map Aadhaar to NPCI" (National Payments Corporation of India).

Solution: Visit your bank branch and submit a "DBT Mandate Form." Government money only goes to the bank account mapped with NPCI.

5. How do I know if my Aadhaar is still active? Can it be deactivated?

Answer: Yes, Aadhaar can be deactivated if biometrics are not updated for children, or if mixed biometrics are detected.

Solution: Use the "Verify Aadhaar" service on the UIDAI website. Enter your number; if it shows a green tick, your Aadhaar is active.
